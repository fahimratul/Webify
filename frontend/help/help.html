Help & Guide

Understand what WEBIFY is, why it exists, and how to use it effectively.

Local-First Open Source No-Code

What is WEBIFY?

WEBIFY is a local-first, open-source static website builder that runs entirely in your browser, letting you create clean personal websites without logins, servers, or subscriptions.

The tool focuses on students, freelancers, and creators who want simple portfolio or resume sites with full privacy and control over their content.

Problem WEBIFY Solves

  • Most builders are subscription-based, bloated, or locked behind cloud accounts.
  • Static site generators often require coding knowledge and complex setups.
  • Privacy-friendly, no-code tools for simple personal sites are rare.

Background & Motivation

  • Personal sites remain important for portfolios, resumes, and self-branding.
  • Many users want to avoid monthly payments and vendor lock‑in.
  • WEBIFY follows a “free forever, support when you can” philosophy, with an optional community marketplace for paid templates and assets.

Project Objectives

  • Provide a simple, no-code visual editor that runs fully in the browser.
  • Export clean static HTML, CSS, and JS ready for any static host.
  • Offer optional local AI assistance for writing and layout suggestions.
  • Respect user privacy with a local-first, offline-friendly design.

Key Features (MVP)

  • Drag-and-drop editor: Add text, images, buttons, headers, footers, and containers with live preview.
  • Local-first storage: Projects saved in your browser (IndexedDB / LocalStorage) with offline editing.
  • Static export: Generate ready-to-deploy HTML/CSS/JS for GitHub Pages, Netlify, and more.
  • Starter templates: Portfolio, resume, and landing-page layouts you can customize easily.
  • Community marketplace: Optional marketplace for sharing or purchasing templates and UI components.

Who Is WEBIFY For?

  • Students and job seekers who need a quick, clean personal site.
  • Freelancers and designers who want lightweight, static portfolios.
  • Developers who prefer open-source tools and static outputs.
  • Privacy-conscious users avoiding cloud-only site builders.
  • Educators teaching web fundamentals with visual tools.

Getting Started (Quick Steps)

  1. Click Create New Project on the dashboard.
  2. Drag text, images, buttons, and layout blocks into your canvas.
  3. Customize colors, fonts, and spacing in the side panel.
  4. Use the optional assistant for copywriting or layout suggestions.
  5. Export static files and upload them to your preferred hosting provider.
